Research Interests: Zoonotic cryptosporidiosis, life cycle and pathogenic mechanisms of apicomplexan parasites, development of molecular diagnostic tools, parasite energy metabolism and novel drug targets.

Major Academic Contributions:

  1. In apicomplexan parasites, identified for the first time that the Cryptosporidium micronemal protein CpTSP4 is transported and secreted via microtubule-based kinesin-dependent trafficking, and elucidated its interaction with host cells. This work was published in mBio.
  2. Established, for the first time in the Cryptosporidium field, a novel TaqMan qRT-PCR/qPCR assay that enables quantitative determination of oocyst viability for zoonotic Cryptosporidium parvum and C. hominis. This method opens a new avenue for viability assessment of Cryptosporidium oocysts and was published in Water Research.
