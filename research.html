Biochemical Metabolism & Drug Development

Our research focuses on identifying and validating essential metabolic pathways in Cryptosporidium and related parasites as potential drug targets. We employ biochemical, genetic, and pharmacological approaches to discover novel anti-parasitic compounds.

Host-Parasite Interaction

We study the molecular mechanisms underlying parasite invasion, establishment, and persistence in host cells. Our work aims to understand how these parasites evade host immune responses and identify vulnerabilities that can be targeted for therapeutic intervention.

Oocyst Enrichment & Detection

Developing improved methods for the detection and quantification of Cryptosporidium oocysts in environmental and clinical samples. Our research focuses on enhancing sensitivity, specificity, and throughput of detection platforms.

Omics-based Analysis

Utilizing genomic, transcriptomic, and proteomic approaches to understand parasite biology at a systems level. We integrate multi-omics data to identify key regulatory networks and biological processes essential for parasite survival and pathogenesis.
